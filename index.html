About the Temple

ఆలయం గురించి
🪷

🏛️ History & Origin

The Kadiri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple, also known as Yogananda Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple, dates to the glorious Vijayanagara Empire era (14th–16th century CE). The East Raja Gopuram was built in 1545 by Achyutha Devaraya, who also endowed the temple with shrines for Lord Vinayaka, Lord Krishna, and a Raghavendra Vrundavana.

The presiding deity, Yogananda Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy, is enshrined in a rare and uniquely serene seated yogic posture (Yoga Narasimha) with Goddess Lakshmi on the divine lap — a form unique in South India. The idol is revered as Swayambhu — self-manifested, having emerged from the roots of the sacred Kadiri tree on the hillock.

⛰️ Sacred Hillock — Kadiri Betta

The temple crowns Kadiri Betta, a rocky hillock rising majestically above the plains of Anantapur district. Pilgrims ascend stone-paved steps flanked by ancient sculptures and dense greenery, culminating in the divine darshan of the Lord.

"ఉగ్రం వీరం మహావిష్ణుం జ్వలంతం సర్వతోముఖం — నృసింహం భీషణం భద్రం మృత్యుమృత్యుం నమామ్యహం"
Narasimha Kavacham — "I bow to Narasimha, fierce and brave, blazing in all directions, who is death to death itself."

Festivals & Events

పండుగలు మరియు ఉత్సవాలు
🪷
🏆

Brahmotsavam

10-Day Grand Festival | Feb–Mar

The most celebrated annual festival drawing lakhs of pilgrims. The Lord is taken in grand procession on Garuda, Hamsa, Ashwam, and other divine vahanas. Cultural programmes, religious discourses, and annadanam are conducted throughout the 10 days.

🦁

Narasimha Jayanti

Vaishakha Shukla Chaturdashi | May

The divine appearance day of Lord Narasimha. Special pujas, abhishekam, night-long bhajans, and a grand night procession mark this most auspicious occasion. Thousands observe fasting and special prayers.

🌿

Vaikunta Ekadasi

Margashira Shukla Ekadasi | Dec–Jan

The Vaikunta Dwaram (Gate of Heaven) is opened at dawn. Devotees who pass through are believed to attain moksha. The night resounds with Divya Prabandham recitation and special golden alankaram.

🌙

Karthika Masam

November

The entire sacred month is celebrated with daily deepotsavam — a sea of lamps illuminating Kadiri Betta. The sight of thousands of diyas glowing on the hilltop at dusk is a breathtaking spiritual experience.

🎪

Rathotsavam

During Brahmotsavam

The grand chariot procession through the streets of Kadiri town. Thousands of devotees pull the colossal wooden chariot adorned with the Lord in magnificent jewellery and flower garlands.

🌸

Teppotsavam

Float Festival | Post Brahmotsavam

The float festival where the deity is taken on a beautifully decorated float on the sacred temple tank, surrounded by thousands of lamps reflecting on the water in an enchanting spiritual spectacle.
